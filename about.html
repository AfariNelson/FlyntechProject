HISTORY

St. Elizabeth School was established as a pre-school by Mr. Bernard Kyeremeh and his late wife (Ms. Elizabeth Tetteh) on 13'h "January 1997 with a school population of five children and one teacher. On g'* September 1997 a primary department was established with a population of five pupils and one teacher. By January 1998 the school's population had risen to ninety-seven pupils with seven teachers. By September 2002, pupils who were enrolled in class one in 1997 had gotten to class six and were fifteen in number. On 22 September 2003, the J.H.S. department was established with eleven J.H.S. one students. In April 2006, this very class sat for the Basic Education Certificate Examination (B.E.C.E) and passed with a record hundred percent. • Currently, the school has a population of four hundred and twenty five pupils, twenty seven teachers and three non-teaching staff. The school was named after a daughter of a King of Hungary who lived in the 13th ' and 14th century. She was canonized as a saint by the Pope because of her love and care for the poor, sick and the needy. (Long Live St. Elizabeth School, Long Live Ghana)
